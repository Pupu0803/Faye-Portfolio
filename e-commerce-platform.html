Momoisland was an e-commerce startup focused on selling Asian products to international customers. I joined as the sole designer and was responsible for all design efforts, including marketing, branding, websites, and eventually the mobile app.

My Role
• Sole UI/UX Designer
• Owned end-to-end design across web and mobile
• Delivered marketing, branding, and product design
Introduction
At the early stage, the company did not yet have a functional app. To support business operations, we needed a fast solution to launch online sales while preparing for a mobile platform.
An external agency had been working on the app for several months but failed to deliver usable results.
Logo
Brand Color
The main color theme for Momo's logo is orange-pink, complemented by a low-saturation coffee-colored font. This design choice is inspired by the brand founder's preference for a cute and vibrant girlish style.

Challenges
• Tight timeline (approx. 6 weeks to deliver the app)
• Taking over an incomplete project from an external agency
• Designing across multiple platforms simultaneously (web + app + marketing)
• Balancing speed and usability
Framwork
After the external agency failed to deliver, I took over the app design and rebuilt it from scratch:
• Defined user workflows
• Created wireframes and prototypes
• Delivered final UI design
Given the tight deadline, I prioritized speed while maintaining usability. I focused on essential features and avoided unnecessary complexity.

Summary
The app was successfully launched and used by customers. The company later shut down due to business reasons, but the project demonstrated the ability to deliver under pressure and take ownership of a full product experience.
What I Learned
• Speed and execution are critical in early-stage startups
• Rebuilding from failed work requires strong structure and decision-making
• End-to-end ownership improves product consistency
