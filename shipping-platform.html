Background
Shipping for businesses is often fragmented and inefficient. Companies rely on manual coordination, phone calls, and disconnected systems to manage deliveries.
V-ship aimed to build a centralized platform that works like a “daily operating system” for shipping—similar to how office tools support daily workflows.
Challenges
• Complex multi-role system (business users, drivers, internal staff)
• Translating real-world logistics into digital workflows
• Handling edge cases (delays, route changes, scheduling conflicts)
• Tight collaboration with external developers under uncertainty

Summary
The platform design was completed and aligned with real business workflows. Although the product did not fully launch due to external development issues, the project provided deep experience in designing complex systems and managing uncertainty.
I also handled unexpected challenges with external vendors, ensuring continuity and mitigating risks.
What I Learned
• Designing for real-world operations requires deep system thinking
• Edge cases define the quality of a product
• Strong ownership is critical in early-stage startups
